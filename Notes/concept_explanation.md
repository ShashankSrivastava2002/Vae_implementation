## Part A — What we were doing (problem statement)

### A.1 Generative story

We assume each observed datapoint \(x\) (e.g. an MNIST image) is generated by:

1. Sample latent code from a **prior**: \(z \sim p(z)\) (standard choice: \(\mathcal{N}(0, I)\)).  
2. Sample (or model) the image given \(z\): **likelihood** \(p_\theta(x \mid z)\), implemented as a **decoder** neural network with parameters \(\theta\).

We never observe \(z\) directly — it is a **latent variable** (historically: factor analysis, PCA, mixture models; the paper’s novelty is **scalable training**, not the idea of latents).

### A.2 Training goal (in principle)

**Maximum likelihood:** choose \(\theta\) (and, in the VAE, also the encoder) so that real data is assigned high probability:

\[
\max_\theta \; \log p(x) \quad \text{for } x \sim \text{data.}
\]

### A.3 Why this is hard (intractability)

By Bayes’ rule, the **true posterior** is:

\[
p(z \mid x) = \frac{p(x \mid z)\, p(z)}{p(x)}.
\]

The denominator is the **marginal likelihood**:

\[
p(x) = \int p(x \mid z)\, p(z)\, dz.
\]

For expressive \(p_\theta(x \mid z)\) (e.g. a neural net), this integral has **no closed form** in general: we cannot evaluate \(p(x)\) or \(p(z \mid x)\) exactly. Classical options like MCMC **do not scale** to large datasets and high dimensions.

**That is the core problem:** we want to train a deep generative model with latents, but **exact inference** is intractable.

## Part B — Solution we “clicked” (high level)

Instead of computing \(p(z \mid x)\):

1. **Introduce an approximate posterior** \(q_\phi(z \mid x)\), implemented by an **encoder** (\(\phi\)) that outputs distribution parameters for \(z\) given \(x\).  
2. **Optimize a tractable lower bound** on \(\log p(x)\) — the **ELBO** (evidence lower bound) — instead of \(\log p(x)\) itself.  
3. Use the **reparameterization trick** so we can **sample** \(z \sim q_\phi(z \mid x)\) while still **backpropagating** through \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\).

Standard concrete choices (first VAE recipe):

- \(p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0, I)\).  
- \(q_\phi(z \mid x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_\phi(x), \mathrm{diag}(\sigma_\phi^2(x)))\).  
- **Reconstruction term:** often MSE or Bernoulli logits, depending on \(p_\theta(x \mid z)\) modeling choice.  
- **KL term:** for Gaussians vs standard normal, **closed form** — no Monte Carlo needed for KL.

## Part C — Mathematical steps of the solution

### C.1 Prerequisites (minimal glossary)

- **Conditional / joint / marginal:** \(p(z \mid x)\), \(p(x, z)\), \(p(x) = \int p(x \mid z)p(z)\,dz\).  
- **KL divergence** \(\mathrm{KL}(q \,\|\, p)\): non-negative, asymmetric measure of how \(q\) differs from \(p\).  
- **Expectation** \(\mathbb{E}_q[\cdot]\): average under samples from \(q\).  
- **Jensen’s inequality** for **concave** \(\log\): \(\log \mathbb{E}[Y] \ge \mathbb{E}[\log Y]\) — used to turn an intractable \(\log\) of an integral into a bound involving an expectation.

*(Longer intuition and examples: `vae_mathematics.md`, Section 1.)*

### C.2 ELBO derivation (sketch)

Start from \(\log p(x)\) and introduce any **variational** density \(q(z \mid x)\) (in practice \(q_\phi(z \mid x)\)):

\[
\log p(x)
= \log \int p(x, z)\,dz
= \log \int q(z \mid x)\, \frac{p(x, z)}{q(z \mid x)}\,dz
= \log \mathbb{E}_{q}\!\left[\frac{p(x, z)}{q(z \mid x)}\right].
\]

Because \(\log\) is concave, Jensen gives:

\[
\log p(x) \ge \mathbb{E}_{q}\!\left[\log \frac{p(x, z)}{q(z \mid x)}\right]
= \mathbb{E}_{q}[\log p(x \mid z)] - \mathrm{KL}(q_\phi(z \mid x) \,\|\, p(z)).
\]

**Right-hand side = ELBO.** Maximizing ELBO:

- pushes up **expected log-likelihood** (good reconstructions / decoder);  
- pushes down **KL** to the prior (regularized, “organized” latents suitable for sampling \(z \sim p(z)\)).

The **gap** between \(\log p(x)\) and ELBO is related to how well \(q_\phi\) matches the true \(p(z \mid x)\).

*(Full step-by-step: `vae_mathematics.md`, Section 3; paper framing: `vae_paper_notes.md`, Section 5.)*

### C.3 Reparameterization trick

Need \(z \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2)\) with gradients w.r.t. \(\mu, \sigma\). **Equivalence:**

\[
z = \mu + \sigma \odot \epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I).
\]

\(\epsilon\) is “external” noise; \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\) are deterministic **paths** — gradients flow through them. Same distribution as direct sampling, but **differentiable** Monte Carlo gradient for the reconstruction term.

*(Proof sketch: `vae_mathematics.md`, Section 3 Step 4 and Section 4 Q “Prove z = μ + σ ε”.).*

### C.4 Gaussian KL vs \(\mathcal{N}(0, I)\) (diagonal)

For \(q = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \mathrm{diag}(\sigma^2))\) and \(p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0, I)\), per latent dimension:

\[
\mathrm{KL}(q \,\|\, p) = -\tfrac{1}{2}\sum_j \bigl(1 + \log \sigma_j^2 - \mu_j^2 - \sigma_j^2\bigr).
\]

In code, \(\log \sigma^2\) is often stored as `log_var`. This term is computed **analytically** (no sampling).

### C.5 Loss used in our notebook (mapping)

**Objective:** minimize \(\text{reconstruction loss} + \text{KL loss}\) (equivalent to maximizing ELBO up to sign conventions).

- Reconstruction: scaled **sum of squared errors** (Gaussian observation model with variance related to `noise` hyperparameter — see `GaussianELBOLoss` in `VAE.ipynb`).  
- KL: matches the analytic diagonal Gaussian form above (implementation details and possible `/2` typo are discussed in `doubts.md`).

## Part F — Full paper trajectory (checklist)

1. **Setup:** \(z \sim p(z)\), \(x \sim p_\theta(x \mid z)\).  
2. **Goal:** maximize \(\log p(x)\) (MLE).  
3. **Blocker:** marginal \(p(x)\) integral intractable \(\Rightarrow\) \(p(z \mid x)\) intractable.  
4. **Variational approximation:** \(q_\phi(z \mid x)\) (Gaussian encoder).  
5. **Objective:** maximize ELBO = \(\mathbb{E}_q[\log p(x \mid z)] - \mathrm{KL}(q_\phi \,\|\, p(z))\).  
6. **Estimator:** Monte Carlo for recon term with **reparameterized** \(z\).  
7. **KL:** analytic for diagonal Gaussian vs \(\mathcal{N}(0,I)\).  
8. **Optimization:** SGD/Adam on \(\phi\) and \(\theta\) jointly.  
9. **Use:** sample \(z \sim p(z)\), decode new \(x\); interpolate or vary posterior samples.
